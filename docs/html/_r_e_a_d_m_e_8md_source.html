1 # Research Track I - second assignment
2 
3 In this second assignment, we were asked to develop a software architecture for controlling a robot within a specific environment.
4 More in details, our software will rely on two different packages:
5 
6 1. the move_base pakage
7 2. the gmapping package
8 
9 The first one, will take care of the localization of our robot within the enviroment, whereas the second one will plan the motion.
10 
11 ## User request ##
12 
13 The architecture will provide a way to get the **user request**, and will make the robot capable of executing one of the following behaviors (depending on the user’s input):
14 
15 1. Move randomly in the environment, by choosing 1 out of 6 possible target position
16  Position | Coordinates
17  ------------ | -------------
18  Position 1 | (-4,-3)
19  Position 2 | (-4,2)
20  Position 3 | (-4,7)
21  Position 4 | (5,-7)
22  Position 5 | (5,-3)
23  Position 6 | (5,1)
24 
25 2. Directly ask the user for the **next** target position (checking that the position is one of the possible six) and reach it)
26 3. Start following the external walls
27 4. Stop in the last position
28 5. (optional) change the planning algorithm to _dijkstra_ (move_base) to the `bug0`
29

34 ## Messages
35 
36 ### Published messages
37 
38 In the list below, the **published messages** of the whole architecture are reported:
39 
40 * **actionlib_msgs/GoalID**: It publishes the messages to the **/move_base/cancel** topic in order to remove a target as it is considered reached. Indeed, it allows to easily avoid the inadvertent overlying of robot _distinct_ behaviours
41 
42 * **geometry_msgs/Twist**: It publishes the messages to the **/cmd_vel** topic. Moreover, it is used:
43  1. for setting both the robot linear and and angular velocity
44  2. for halting the robot.
45 
46 * **move_base_msgs/MoveBaseActionGoal**: It publishes the messages to the **/move_base/goal** topic. Moreover, it is used for setting the move_base goal that the robot has to achieve
47 
48 ### Subscribed messages
49 
50 In the list below, the **subscribed messages** of the whole architecture are reported:
51 
52 * **geometry_msgs/Point**: It defines the robot position, expressed as a **Point**
53 
54 * **nav_msgs/Odometry**: It provides the current robot position by means of the **/Odom** topic
55 
56 * **sensor_msgs/LaserScanl** It provides the real-time laser output through the **/scan** topic

58 ## Services
59 
60 As far as services are concerned, I have here-reported a quick explanation of their purposes:
61 
62 1. Within the [random_srv.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/random_srv.py) script, there is the `/random_srv` service. Its purpose is to choose between six different coordinate positions
63 
64 2. Within the [go_to_point_service_m.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/go_to_point_service_m.py) script, there is the `/go_to_point_switch` service. Its purpose is to _activate/deactivate_ the **go-to-point** behaviour
65 
66 3. Within the [ui.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/ui.py) script, we find the `/ui` service. its purpose consists in providing a tool for selecting one out of the **five** possible robot states. In the table below, it is possible to spot what, the different states implement:
67 
68 _state_ | _Coordinates_
69 ------------ | -------------
70 **state 1** | move randomly
71 **state 2** | target position
72 **state 3** | walls following
73 **state 4** | stop
74 **state 5** | bug algorithm
75 
76 > _REMARK:_ If `state 5` is chosen, I have implemented a sort of _timer_ which allows the user to pick up another new target by means of a user-interface if an inserted target is not reachable. Once the target position has been reached or once the settled timer's time (20 secs, in my implementation) has expired, the program exits the bug state
77 
78 4. Within the [user_interface.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/user_interface.py) script, we find the `/user_interface service`. Its purpose basically consists in providing a tool for selecting the _x,y coordinates_ of the next desired robot target position, once the previous target has been reached. Moreover, this is the _user-interface_ exploited by the **bug algorithm**
79 
80 5. Within the [wall_follower_service_m.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/wall_follower_service_m.py) script, we find the `/wall_follower_switch` service. Its purpose is to _activate/deactivate_ the **wall-follower** behaviour
81 
82 6. Within the [wall_follower_bug.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/wall_follower_bug.py) script, we find the `/wall_follower_bug` service.
83 Its purpose is to _activate/deactivate_ the **wall-follower** behaviour.
84 > _REMARK:_ This is the service exploited by the `bug algorithm`!
85 
86 7. Within the [bug_m.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/bug_m.py) script, we find the `/bug_alg` service. Its purpose is to _activate/deactivate_ the **bug algorithm** behaviour (through a boolean value)

88 ## Nodes
89 
90 Within the [scripts](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts) folder, the following nodes are available:
91 
92 1. [master_node.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/master_node.py)
93 It implements the structure of the entire architecture. It handles the simulation structure and it provides a way for checking the robot state. Then, it triggers the required service.
94 
95 2. [user_interface.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/user_interface.py)
96 As its name suggests, It provides a service, exploited by the **bug algorithm**,for letting the user choose which **target position** the robot should achieve.
97 
98 3. [ui.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/ui.py)
99 It provides a user-interface in which the user has to select 1 out of 5 available states. Then, once inserted the chosen state, a "valid state" **message** appears, so that the user can save his/her choice by typing the string "done"
100 > _REMARK:_ When entering the "done" string or other strings, remember to add the quote marks ""
101 
102 4. [wall_follower_bug.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/wall_follower_bug.py)
103 It provides a service for avoiding the collision between our robot and the neighboring walls while performing the target achievement
104 > _REMARK:_ We need to distinguish between _wall_follower_bug.py_ and _wall_follower_service_m.py_ because even if they share the same code structure, when calling the latter service, both 'bug algorithm' and its _wall_follower_service_m_ are disabled!
105 
106 5. [wall_follower_service_m.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/wall_follower_service_m.py)
107 As its name suggests, it provides a service for simulating the wall following behaviour
108 
109 6. [go_to_point_service_m.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/go_to_point_service_m.py)
110 It provides a service for making the **bug algorithm*** capable of implementing the **go-to-point** behaviour
111 
112 7. [random_srv.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/random_srv.py)
113 It provides a straightforward service, for setting a **random position** which the robot will achieve
114 
115 8. [bug_m.py](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/scripts/bug_m.py)
116 It provides a service for carrying out the **bug algorithm** behaviour

118 ## Parameters
119 
120 They are contained in the [launch](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/tree/main/launch) folder. More specifically:
121 
122 * Within the [node_master.launch](https://github.com/fedehub/final_assignment/blob/main/launch/node_master.launch) file, I have initialised:
123  * `des_pos_x` and `des_pos_y` for allocating the _x,y coordinates_ of the target the user has chosen (so that the robot can reach them)
124  * `state` for individuating the current state (between _1 and 5_) of the robot
125  * `change_state` for initialising at _zero_ the current state of our robot. It has been proven very useful during the implementation of the **bug algorithm**
